# Contributing

Compai is a build-in-public project. The whole point is that other operators copy this, adapt it, and improve it. Contributions are welcome — and so is just telling us what you built.

## Three ways to contribute

### 1. Share what you built

This is the most valuable thing you can do. If you forked the playbook, adapted a pattern, or ran any of this inside your own company, open a "Share what you built" issue. We keep a public list of real deployments, and your notes help the next operator.

You don't need a polished writeup. One paragraph on what worked, what broke, and what you'd change is gold.

### 2. Improve the playbook

The chapters, patterns, and starter skills are living documents. Good contributions:

- Fix an error, a stale number, or a broken link.
- Clarify a chapter that confused you.
- Add a pattern you validated in production (anonymized).
- Improve a starter skill or template.
- Translate a chapter.

Open a pull request. Keep changes focused — one idea per PR.

### 3. Report a problem

- Found a bug in the kit or a script? Open a Bug report.
- Have a question the playbook didn't answer? Open a Question.
- Found a security issue? See SECURITY.md — do **not** open a public issue.

## What belongs here

This repo is an **educational implementation portfolio**, not a product. Contributions should help someone understand or build the operating model.

**Good:** patterns, contracts, anonymized lessons, kit improvements, clarifications, translations.

**Out of scope:** anything tied to a specific company's private data — credentials, real Slack channels, customer records, HR data, source-system tokens, or anything you wouldn't want a stranger to read. If you contribute a pattern, anonymize it first.

## Style

- Honest over impressive. If something took 8 weeks, say 8 weeks.
- Numbers with their assumptions. No "10x" without the math.
- Plain language. The reader is a non-technical operator who is technical enough to deploy.
- No vendor marketing. Describe what you ran, not what you hope.

## Pull request checklist

- [ ] One focused change.
- [ ] No private data, credentials, or real account names.
- [ ] Claims have evidence or are clearly marked as opinion.
- [ ] Stale numbers updated if you touched them.
