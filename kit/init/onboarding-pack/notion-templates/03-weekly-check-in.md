# Weekly AI check-in — {BRAND}

*Run every Friday, 15 min, solo or with your manager.*

The point of this check-in is NOT to report. It's to reinforce the habit and catch drift early. Fill in your row in the team's AI Progress Sheet (or use this as a personal log).

## The 5 questions

### 1. Adoption level this week
- [ ] **L0** — I didn't meaningfully use Claude this week
- [ ] **L1** — Claude was my go-to for looking things up
- [ ] **L2** — I built or customized something (skill, SOUL edit, workflow hook, custom prompt)
- [ ] **L3** — I taught someone else something, or my work made another teammate's Claude better

Where are you today? Where were you last week?

### 2. Wins
What's one thing Claude did for you this week that would have taken >30 min without it?

*Example: "Drafted 8 refund replies in <5 min total — would have been 40 min typing myself"*

### 3. Losses
What's one thing Claude got wrong this week? What would have fixed it?

*Example: "Gave me Q2 revenue number from memory instead of `shopify_query` — should trigger /learn to reinforce the rule"*

### 4. /learn count
How many `/learn` commands did you run this week?

Target: ≥2/week. If <2, you're probably not ending sessions with enough intention — the system only compounds if we capture learnings.

### 5. One question for the team
What's one thing you wish the brain had documented that it doesn't?

*Goes into the team's pending-docs backlog. If 3 people ask for the same thing, someone writes it.*

## Progress markers

Fill these in monthly, not weekly:

### Month 1
- [ ] Cold-start muscle memory: default to Claude before Google/Slack/asking a colleague
- [ ] me.md created and at least once updated
- [ ] Custom instruction pasted in Claude Desktop

### Month 3
- [ ] At least 1 skill I've written or customized is in regular use by someone besides me
- [ ] My `/learn` count is ≥20 total
- [ ] I've proposed at least 1 SOUL.md edit to {founder}

### Month 6
- [ ] I can operate comfortably at the abstraction of "brief the swarm, review the result" — I'm not doing the work myself
- [ ] My socioemotional time (% of week spent with humans, not systems) is noticeably higher than 6 months ago
- [ ] At least one teammate is >70% of where I am on Claude fluency because of something I did

### Year 1
- [ ] I've taken a week off and the systems I own didn't break
- [ ] I've introduced at least 1 new agent or factory to the brand
- [ ] My role has been reclassified (M-shaped / T-shaped / AI-Empowered Frontline) or I'm ready for reclassification

## Don't gamify

These markers are observations, not quotas. Don't run /learn just to hit 2/week. Don't write a skill just to cross off a box. The goal is real work done better, not a progress bar.

If you're at Month 3 and still feel L1, that's fine — tell {founder}, do a pair session with someone at L2+. The framework is a map, not a race.
